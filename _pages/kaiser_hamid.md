Kaiser Hamid is a PhD student at Texas Tech University. He completed his undergraduate studies at the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where he built a strong background in engineering and computational methods. His research interests include autonomous driving, computer vision, multimodal learning, and human-centered AI.

His current work focuses on vision-language-action models, driver attention modeling, interpretability, and robust evaluation of autonomous systems. He is motivated by the challenge of developing autonomous driving systems that are not only high-performing, but also more understandable, reliable, and grounded in human-centered design.

Outside research, he enjoys driving, traveling, and experiencing new places and environments. These interests naturally connect with his academic work and continue to inspire his perspective on mobility, interaction, and intelligent transportation.
