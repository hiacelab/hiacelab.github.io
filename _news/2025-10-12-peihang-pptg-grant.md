HiACE Lab PhD student **Peihang Li**, supervised by **Dr. Nade Liang**, received the **PPTG Student Research Grant** from the **Perception and Performance Technical Group** of the **Human Factors and Ergonomics Society (HFES)**.

The award provides support for Peihang’s ongoing research in **transportation safety and human factors**. As part of the recognition, he was invited to present his work during the PPTG social event and contribute a summary of the study to the PPTG newsletter.

The funded proposal, **“Risk Perception and Driver Adaptation in Dynamic Traffic Environments,”** investigates how drivers perceive and adapt to transitions in traffic density and speed conditions. Using a driving simulator and physiological measures such as **eye-tracking** and **galvanic skin response**, the study examines how perceived risk influences driver behavior and decision-making.
