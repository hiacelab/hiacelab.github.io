HiACE Lab PhD student **Kaiser Hamid**, supervised by **Dr. Nade Liang**, was selected to present his study titled **“Driver Visual Attention Modeling via Vision-Language In-Context Learning”** at the **105th Transportation Research Board (TRB) Annual Meeting**, to be held **January 11–15, 2026**, in **Washington, D.C.**

This research introduces a **vision-language modeling framework** to analyze how human drivers allocate visual attention across dynamic road environments. By combining large-scale visual data with language-driven context reasoning, the study models both **where** and **why** drivers focus their attention under complex traffic scenarios.

The work aims to advance **interpretable driver-attention prediction** and support the design of safer, cognitively aware automated driving systems.

The study was co-authored with **Dr. Khandakar Ashrafi Akbar**.
